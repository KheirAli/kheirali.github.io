I am a PhD student in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Georgia Tech. My research focuses on machine learning for inverse problems, diffusion models, and detecting distribution shifts in partially observed data.

Recently, I have been working on localized out-of-distribution detection using diffusion priors, with applications in computational imaging, medical imaging, and visual anomaly detection.

Research

My work studies how generative priors can help solve inverse problems when the observation may contain unusual or out-of-distribution structure. I am especially interested in methods that do not only reconstruct an image, but also explain where and why the reconstruction may be unreliable.

More broadly, I am interested in diffusion models, posterior sampling, computational imaging, distributed machine learning, and robust machine learning systems.
